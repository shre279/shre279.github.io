About
Shreyansh Mishra

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at BRGM (Bureau de Recherches Géologiques et Minières) in Orléans, France, working on groundwater modelling, hydroeconomic optimization, and machine learning methods for water resource management.

I completed my PhD in Geoinformatics from IIT BHU, where I developed expertise in geospatial data analysis, remote sensing, and hydrological simulation. My current work focuses on building scalable, basin-agnostic methodologies — combining physics-based models with ML surrogates for multi-objective optimization under climate uncertainty.

Outside the lab, I'm interested in Vedic philosophy, Ayurveda, cycling the Loire Valley, and capturing moody frames on my iPhone.
